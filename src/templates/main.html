A one in 100 year sea-level event is by definition relatively rare, with coastal flooding requiring unusual circumstances like the Sygna storm or the 2006 storm which devestated the waterfront at Collaroy.

However, due to climate change the overall sea level is going to rise.

This means that extreme sea level events will happen much more frequently, as everything will be higher - so the sea level heights that previously resulted from a severe storm can happen during much more mild weather and tides.

In this simplified example, what was once a one in 100 event now happens 33 times in 100 - or a frequency similar to once every three years.
